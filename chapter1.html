What is an LLM?

Core Concept

Large Language Models (LLMs) are deep neural networks trained on massive text corpora to understand and generate human language. Think of them as statistical models that predict the next word in a sequence.

The "large" in LLM refers to both parameter count (typically hundreds of millions to trillions) and dataset size (often terabytes of text).

How Do They Work?

Under the Hood

At their core, LLMs work by predicting the next word in a sequence. This prediction is based on patterns learned from massive amounts of text data.
