Historical Evolution

🤖

1950: The Turing Test

Alan Turing proposed the "Imitation Game" - if a machine can engage in conversations indistinguishable from a human, it demonstrates intelligence.

🧪 Try the Turing Test Yourself!

Chat with this bot - can you tell if it's human or machine?

Bot: Hello! I'm here to chat. Ask me anything you'd like!

💡 Try asking about hobbies, feelings, or personal experiences
💬

1966: ELIZA

Created by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT, ELIZA was one of the first natural language processing programs. It used pattern matching to simulate conversation, famously acting as a psychotherapist.

User: My boyfriend told me to talk to you.

ELIZA: Your boyfriend told you to talk to me?

User: He says I'm often depressed.

ELIZA: I'm sorry to hear you are depressed.

User: That's true. I feel unhappy.

ELIZA: Do you think talking to me will help you not feel unhappy?

😤

1972: PARRY

Developed by Kenneth Colby, PARRY simulated a paranoid patient, representing early attempts at creating more sophisticated conversational agents.

Historic AI vs AI Conversation (1972)

PARRY: People get on my nerves sometimes.
ELIZA: I am not sure I understand you fully.
PARRY: You should pay more attention.
ELIZA: Suppose you should pay more attention.
PARRY: You're entitled to your own opinion.
ELIZA: What makes you think I am entitled to my own opinion?
PARRY: It bothers me just to be around people in general.
First recorded conversation between two AI programs! 🤖💬🤖

1988: Statistical Language Models

The shift from rule-based to statistical approaches using n-grams and hidden Markov models for language processing.

1997: LSTM Networks

Long Short-Term Memory networks enable better sequence modeling and capture long-range dependencies in text.

⚡

2017: Transformer Architecture

"Attention Is All You Need" - Revolutionary breakthrough that enabled all modern LLMs by allowing models to focus on relevant parts of text simultaneously.

2018: GPT-1

117M parameters, introduced the concept of pre-training on large text corpora and demonstrated the power of transformer-based language models.

2

2019: GPT-2

1.5B parameters, demonstrated impressive text generation capabilities and sparked debates about AI safety and misuse.

3

2020: GPT-3

175B parameters, showed remarkable few-shot learning abilities and could perform tasks with minimal examples.

💬

2022: ChatGPT

Based on GPT-3.5, brought LLMs to mainstream attention through conversational interface and human-like responses.

2023: GPT-4 & Claude

Multimodal capabilities emerged, with models able to process both text and images. GPT-4 achieved near-human performance on many benchmarks, while Claude focused on safety and helpfulness.

🔓

2024: Open Source Revolution

Models like LLaMA 3, Mistral, and Gemma made high-quality LLMs accessible to researchers and developers worldwide, democratizing AI development.

🔮

2025: The Future

Anticipated developments include GPT-5, improved reasoning capabilities, better memory systems, and more efficient training methods.
