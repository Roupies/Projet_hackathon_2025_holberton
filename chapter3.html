Historical Evolution

üí¨

1966: ELIZA

Created by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT, ELIZA was one of the first natural language processing programs. It used pattern matching to simulate conversation, famously acting as a psychotherapist.

User: Men are all alike.

ELIZA: IN WHAT WAY?

User: Well, my boyfriend made me come here.

ELIZA: YOUR BOYFRIEND MADE YOU COME HERE?

1972: PARRY

Developed by Kenneth Colby, PARRY simulated a paranoid patient, representing early attempts at creating more sophisticated conversational agents.

1988: Statistical Language Models

The shift from rule-based to statistical approaches using n-grams and hidden Markov models for language processing.

1997: LSTM Networks

Long Short-Term Memory networks enable better sequence modeling and capture long-range dependencies in text.

Key Trends & Insights

Scaling Laws

Performance improves predictably with model size, following power laws. This has driven the race for larger models and better architectures.

Emergent Abilities

New capabilities appear suddenly at certain model sizes, such as few-shot learning, reasoning, and code generation.

Efficiency Improvements

Better architectures and training methods have made models more efficient, reducing computational requirements.

Safety & Alignment

As models become more capable, research has focused on ensuring they behave safely and align with human values.
