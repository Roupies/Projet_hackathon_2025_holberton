1966: ELIZA

Created by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT, ELIZA was one of the first natural language processing programs. It used pattern matching to simulate conversation, famously acting as a psychotherapist.

User: My boyfriend told me to talk to you.

ELIZA: Your boyfriend told you to talk to me?

User: He says I'm often depressed.

ELIZA: I'm sorry to hear you are depressed.

User: That's true. I feel unhappy.

ELIZA: Do you think talking to me will help you not feel unhappy?

1972: PARRY

Developed by Kenneth Colby, PARRY simulated a paranoid patient, representing early attempts at creating more sophisticated conversational agents.

Historic AI vs AI Conversation (1972)

PARRY: People get on my nerves sometimes.
ELIZA: I am not sure I understand you fully.
PARRY: You should pay more attention.
ELIZA: Suppose you should pay more attention.
PARRY: You're entitled to your own opinion.
ELIZA: What makes you think I am entitled to my own opinion?
PARRY: It bothers me just to be around people in general.

117M parameters, introduced the concept of pre-training on large text corpora and demonstrated the power of transformer-based language models.

2

2019: GPT-2

1.5B parameters, demonstrated impressive text generation capabilities and sparked debates about AI safety and misuse.

3

2020: GPT-3

175B parameters, showed remarkable few-shot learning abilities and could perform tasks with minimal examples.
